What is an atom?
ans An atom is a general-purpose name with no inherent meaning. It is composed of a sequence of characters that is parsed by the Prolog reader as a single unit.

What is a term?
ans True when Term is a term with functor Name / Arity . If Term is a variable it is unified with a new term whose arguments are all different variables.

What is functor?
ans In Prolog, the word functor is used to refer to the atom at the start of a structure, along with its arity, that is, the number of arguments it takes.

What are complex terms?
ans The number of arguments that a complex term has is called its arity.

What is predicate?
ans A predicate denotes a property or relationship between objects. A clause has a head and a body (Rule) or just a head (Fact).

What is arity of predicate?
ans A predicate with name Pred and N arguments is denoted by Pred/N, which is called a predicate indicator. N is called the arity of the predicate.

What is unification?
ans The way in which Prolog matches two terms is called unification.
